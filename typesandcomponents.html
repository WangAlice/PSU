Types and components (and how to choose a good PSU)

Modular vs Semi-modular vs Non-modular

Modular Semi-modular Non-modular
What is it A modular PSU is one that comes only with ports, and wires. The user is able to use and change chords when desired. A semi-modular PSU is one that comes with a certain set of chords that are not removable, and others that are, allowing for some freedom. A non modular PSU is one that already comes with a specific set of chords. You are not able to remove or add new chords.

    Things to consider when buying:

    1. Your PSU should be one of the last things you want to buy. They are less customizable then the other parts
    2. Check your total watts required on PCpartpicker.com. For better security, multiply that number by 1.5, just in case your PC goes over.
    3. Though the vast majority of motherboards only support ATX, it is always good to check to make sure, because other configurations such as SFX or TXF.
